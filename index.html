I am a masters student in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering and an active researcher in Cyber-Security. Currently, I am working with Haadi Jafarian, Assistant Professor and the Director of Active Cyber and Infrastructure Defense (ACID) lab at the University of Colorado Denver. I received my B.Tech. from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Patna in 2017 where I was a researcher in Applied Cryptography, specifically Password Security and Privacy in Human-Computer Interaction(HCI) Authentication Systems.

My research interests encompass a wide range of topics, mostly in the field of Mathematics and Computer Science, but not limited to Security and Privacy. I am also quite interested in other fields of Computer Science such as Software-Defined Networks, Deep Learning, Image processing, Internet of Things, etc.

Research Projects

User-Authentication

  • MobSecure: Designed a Shoulder-Surfing Resilient Password Authentication Protocol for Android Devices / Windows PC.

  • Dual Gate: Designed a Two-Layered User - Authentication Protocol, Arranged in The Form of a Distributed System to Detect and Trap Adverseries and at the same time Facilitating Password Re-use.

Cryptographic Protocols

  • Distribution Tamper: Designed a Lossy Transformation and Zero-Loss Recovery Protocol to Withstand Frequency Attacks on Substitution Ciphers.

  • FIRE: Designed a Highly Secure, Flexible length, Light-Weight Hybrid Stream Encryption Algorithm that outruns all existing Block and Stream ciphers.

  • Obfuscating Encryption: Designed a new method of plain-text encryption to decieve adversaries running brute-force attacks on leaked offline database.

Network Security

  • Worked with Dr Haadi Jafarian and designed a fast random scanning algorithm that is uniform without repetition.
